## The Financial System / 金融體系

**EN**

The financial system can be viewed from two different angles:
- Institutional perspective: the financial system encompasses the markets, intermediaries, instruments, clients etc.; and
- Functional perspective: the financial system facilitates the allocation and deployment of economic resources, across time and space and in uncertain environment.

While its institutional composition changes over time and space (different places on the globe), the basic functions of a financial system are essentially the same in all economies. The institutional dimension results from the functional needs.

**中文**

金融體系可以從兩個不同的角度來看：
- 制度面的觀點：金融體系涵蓋市場、中介機構、金融工具、客戶等；以及
- 功能面的觀點：金融體系有助於跨越時間與空間、並在不確定環境下進行經濟資源的配置與部署。

雖然金融體系的制度組成會隨時間與空間（地球上不同地方）而改變，但金融體系的基本功能在所有經濟體中本質上都是相同的。制度面的構成是源自於功能面的需求。

---

## Functional Perspective / 功能面的觀點

**EN**

The financial system provides six functions:
- Transferring economic resources across time (e.g. student loans, retirement funds) and space (global financial market, e.g. institutional investments).
- Clearing and settling payments to facilitate trades: money, check, ATM cards, mortgages and mortgage interest payments, etc.
- Pooling of resources to undertake large-scale indivisible enterprise (equities, money market funds, mutual funds etc.).
- Information to coordinate decision-making, facilitating information flow, "price discovery", the normal indices for equities, fixed income rates, volatilities etc.
- Dealing with agency problems, mitigate moral hazard, adverse selection and principal-agent based problems that are caused by asymmetry information or incentive mis-matches (collateralization, CEO incentive for company performance etc.).
- Managing risks, bundling, packaging, pooling and tranching of risk (insurance companies, CMO, CDO, exotic derivatives etc.).

**中文**

金融體系提供六項功能：
- 跨時間（例如學生貸款、退休基金）與跨空間（全球金融市場，例如機構投資）轉移經濟資源。
- 清算與結算支付以促進交易：現金、支票、提款卡、房貸與房貸利息支付等。
- 匯集資源以承擔大規模、不可分割的事業（股票、貨幣市場基金、共同基金等）。
- 提供資訊以協調決策、促進資訊流動、「價格發現」，以及股票、固定收益利率、波動率等的常見指標。
- 處理代理問題，減輕由資訊不對稱或誘因錯配所造成的道德風險、逆向選擇與委託－代理問題（擔保品設計、以公司績效為基礎的執行長激勵等）。
- 管理風險，將風險進行捆綁、包裝、匯集與分層（保險公司、CMO、CDO、奇異衍生性商品等）。

## Institutional Perspective / 制度面的觀點

**EN**

The institutional compositions of the financial system includes:
- Financial markets: equity, fixed income (debt), credits and derivatives.
- Financial intermediaries: banks, insurance companies, pension funds, mutual funds, investment banks, venture capital firms, asset management firms and information providers etc.
- Financial infrastructure and regulation: trading rules, contract enforcement, account system, capital requirements etc.
- Governmental and quasi-governmental organizations: SEC, central banks (Federal Reserve System), the Bank for International Settlement (BIS), the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the World Bank, etc.

International markets differ in many ways:
- The US market is currently the only market offering Financial Futures on individual stocks;
- Germany closed the "Neue Markt", eliminating a platform for not very liquid stocks, regarding stock exchanges inexperienced companies, thus rising capital is getting more difficult for young companies;
- Investment attitudes and awareness are subject to national regulations, e.g. pension fund regulation — defined benefit vs. defined contribution plans.

**中文**

金融體系的制度組成包括：
- 金融市場：股票、固定收益（債務）、信用與衍生性商品。
- 金融中介機構：銀行、保險公司、退休基金、共同基金、投資銀行、創投公司、資產管理公司與資訊提供者等。
- 金融基礎建設與法規：交易規則、契約執行、帳戶系統、資本要求等。
- 政府與準政府組織：證券交易委員會（SEC）、各國中央銀行（如美國聯邦準備系統）、國際清算銀行（BIS）、國際貨幣基金（IMF）、世界銀行等。

國際市場在許多方面各不相同：
- 美國市場目前是唯一提供個股金融期貨的市場；
- 德國關閉了「新市場（Neue Markt）」，取消了一個提供流動性不高、屬於對股票交易較無經驗公司的平台，因此年輕公司籌資變得更加困難；
- 投資態度與意識受各國法規影響，例如退休基金法規——確定給付（defined benefit）相對於確定提撥（defined contribution）計畫。

## Stocks / 股票

**EN**

Common stocks, also known as Equities, represent ownership shares of a corporation.
- Two important characteristics: residual claim and limited liability;
- Sources of returns: dividends and capital gains;
- Calculating returns: buy at time 0 and pay P₀, sell at time T and receive Pₜ and dividend Dₜ:
  - The percentage return is calculated as: rₜ = (Pₜ + Dₜ − P₀) / P₀
  - The log-return is calculated as: rₜ = ln[(Pₜ + Dₜ) / P₀]
  - For small rₜ, the log-return and percentage return are close.
  - Question: Why in some situations should we prefer to use log-returns?
- Some determinants of stock returns:
  - Firm-specific condition: management, productivity, earnings, growth potential, market-liquidity.
  - Market condition: market indices (volatility, volume etc.), e.g. Nasdaq, S&P 500, DAX, FTSE etc.
  - Economic condition: macro-economic variables, e.g. GDP-growth, inflation, employment rate, business cycles, liquidity, interest rates etc.
- Some important empirical evidence:
  - Patterns in the cross-section of stock return: value (value vs. growth), size (small vs. large), momentum (low vs. high);
  - Time-series behavior of stock returns: time-varying expected returns, predictability, stochastic volatility etc.

**中文**

普通股，又稱為「權益（Equities）」，代表對一家公司的所有權份額。
- 兩個重要特性：剩餘求償權（residual claim）與有限責任（limited liability）；
- 報酬來源：股利與資本利得；
- 計算報酬：在時點 0 以 P₀ 買入，在時點 T 賣出並收到 Pₜ 與股利 Dₜ：
  - 百分比報酬率計算為：rₜ = (Pₜ + Dₜ − P₀) / P₀
  - 對數報酬率計算為：rₜ = ln[(Pₜ + Dₜ) / P₀]
  - 當 rₜ 很小時，對數報酬與百分比報酬會很接近。
  - 問題：為什麼在某些情況下，我們會比較偏好使用對數報酬？
- 股票報酬的一些決定因素：
  - 公司本身的狀況：管理階層、生產力、盈餘、成長潛力、市場流動性。
  - 市場狀況：市場指數（波動率、成交量等），例如那斯達克、S&P 500、DAX、FTSE 等。
  - 經濟狀況：總體經濟變數，例如 GDP 成長、通膨、就業率、景氣循環、流動性、利率等。
- 一些重要的實證證據：
  - 股票報酬橫斷面（cross-section）的型態：價值（價值股相對於成長股）、規模（小型相對於大型）、動能（低相對於高）；
  - 股票報酬的時間序列行為：隨時間變動的預期報酬、可預測性、隨機波動率等。

**EN**

Fixed income, also known as debt-instruments, promise to pay fixed, pre-determined stream of cash flows in the future: Coupon payments, Principal amount (denominations), Time to maturity, Sinking fund obligations, etc.

固定收益，又稱為「債務工具（debt-instruments）」，承諾在未來支付固定、預先決定的一連串現金流：票息支付、本金金額（面額）、到期期間、償債基金義務等。

## Corporate Bonds / 公司債

**EN**

Corporate bonds are similar in structure to Treasury issues, with one important difference: default risk.
- Because of the default risk, corporate bonds are cheaper (than their respective Treasury counterparts), paying higher yields.
- The difference in yields is referred to as credit spread.
- The probability of default varies across firms of different credit qualities (e.g. countries, industries, guarantees etc.).
- The probability of default varies over time!

Figure 8: Standard & Poor's one year transition matrix, Source: RiskMetrics™ Technical Document, p. 69.

**中文**

公司債在結構上與公債（Treasury）發行類似，但有一個重要差異：違約風險。
- 由於違約風險的存在，公司債（相對於對應的公債）較為便宜，並提供較高的殖利率。
- 殖利率上的差異稱為信用利差（credit spread）。
- 違約機率會因不同信用品質的公司而異（例如不同國家、產業、擔保等）。
- 違約機率也會隨時間而改變！

圖 8：標準普爾（Standard & Poor's）的一年期轉換矩陣，來源：RiskMetrics™ 技術文件，第 69 頁。

## Currencies / 貨幣

**EN**

Currencies are the most liquid financial instrument.

Currency instruments have generally speaking the same type of parameters, however with different characteristics, e.g. currency return volatility, which is not shaped in the same form as the equity return volatility.

Currency positions usually have a much shorter maturity — proprietary traders on Wall Street take positions up to half an hour!

**中文**

貨幣是流動性最高的金融工具。

一般而言，貨幣工具具有相同類型的參數，但特性不同，例如貨幣報酬的波動率，其形態與股票報酬波動率並不相同。

貨幣部位通常的持有期間短得多——華爾街的自營交易員所持有的部位甚至只長達半小時！

## Financial Innovation / 金融創新

**EN**

Does financial innovation add value? If taken to excess, any virtue can readily become a vice: The market has seen examples of failed financial products, erroneous assumption and strategies, abusive usage of derivatives, distressed hedge funds etc. Empirical evidence supports the statement, that financial innovation does provide social wealth:
- It caters to the investment diversity desired by the investors;
- It improves the opportunities for investors to receive efficient risk-return trade-offs;
- It provides risk management tools for all market participants; and
- It promotes broad distribution and liquidity to economic resources.

**中文**

金融創新是否增添價值？任何優點一旦過度，都很容易變成缺點：市場上曾出現失敗的金融商品、錯誤的假設與策略、衍生性商品的濫用、陷入困境的避險基金等案例。實證證據支持以下論點：金融創新確實能創造社會財富：
- 它滿足投資人所期望的投資多樣性；
- 它改善投資人取得有效率風險－報酬權衡的機會；
- 它為所有市場參與者提供風險管理工具；以及
- 它促進經濟資源的廣泛分配與流動性。

## 🔶Summary / 總結

**EN**

What to learn in this course? Analytical modeling skills, quantitative tools, and empirical knowledge about the financial market and the financial instruments.

How to think about the financial system?
- Functional perspectives: across time and space;
- Institutional compositions: result from the functional needs.
- Does financial innovation add value?
- Sometimes abusive usage of financial innovation causes disruptions.
